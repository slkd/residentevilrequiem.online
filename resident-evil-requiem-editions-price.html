Deluxe Edition DLC — Every Item Explained

The Deluxe Edition DLC pack contains 8 categories of bonuses. Here's a breakdown of what each item actually does in-game:

👔 Leon Costumes (×3)

Film Noir — Monochromatic noir-style suit, pairs with the Film Noir screen filter. RE4 — Leon's iconic classic look from Resident Evil 4. Apocalypse — Combat-ready tactical gear.

👗 Grace Costumes (×2)

Dimitrescu — Elegant gothic-inspired outfit referencing Lady Dimitrescu from RE Village. Film Noir — Pairs with the Film Noir screen filter for a atmospheric look.

🔫 Weapon Skins — S&S M232

Custom skins for the S&S M232 revolver in Apocalypse and Film Noir variants. Both are purely cosmetic with no stat changes.

🔫 Weapon Skins — Requiem

Custom skins for the Requiem handgun in Apocalypse and Film Noir variants.

🎞️ Screen Filters (×2)

Apocalypse filter gives the game a gritty, desaturated post-apocalyptic look. Film Noir filter renders the game in classic black-and-white cinematography.

🦝 Accessories — Mr. Raccoon & DSO Badge

Mr. Raccoon — The iconic Raccoon City mascot trinket. DSO Badge — Leon's government agency identification, wearable as an in-game charm.

🎵 Raccoon City Classic Soundtrack

An audio pack that replaces in-game ambient and combat music with classic tracks from Resident Evil 2 (1998) and the original Raccoon City era games.

📄 Secret Letter from 1998

A classified in-game document from 1998 — hinting at events that connect Alyssa Ashcroft's past to the founding of Umbrella's Midwestern operations.

Is the Deluxe Edition Worth $20 More?

The Deluxe Edition costs $89.99 versus the Standard Edition's $69.99 — a $20 premium. Here's a frank breakdown of whether the extras justify the price:

  • For series veterans and lore fans: The Letter from 1998 secret file and the RE4 Leon costume alone make the upgrade worthwhile. The file contains genuine story content that connects Alyssa's past to the broader Resident Evil universe.
  • For completionists: The Film Noir screen filter with matching costumes and weapon skins completely transforms the visual presentation of the game — it's a full second playthrough experience.
  • For casual players: The Standard Edition provides the complete story. If costumes aren't your priority, you lose nothing narratively by skipping the upgrade.
  • Bottom line: The Deluxe Edition is good value for dedicated fans. At $20 extra for 8 categories of DLC, it works out to roughly $2.50 per content category.
